Background

We've been tasked with finding trends that shape peoples's lives based on information from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System. We worked with a 2014 ACS 1-year estimates dataset, which includes data on income rates, obesity, poverty, etc. by state.


Below you will find an interactive chart that allows you to swicth between labels to discover valuable information about the indicators. We have also added some trends and insights regarding this data set for its better use.

Observable trends:

This is an interact graph, click on "labels" and "dots" to see more.

Lack of Healthcare VS Age, Obesity and Poverty: the data show that the age group between 36-40 years old is the one who most lacks healthcare. Although there are outliers in the data, we can see the positive relationship between these indicators. Regarding obesity, there is a strong relation between the lack of healthcare and obesity. The higher the obesity rate, the higher the lack of healthcare. Comparing poverty and lack of healthcare rates, we can also see that they are closely related.

Smokes Rate VS Age, Obesity and Poverty: the data show there is always a relationship between these indicators. While smokes rate and obesity has a positive relationship, smokes and people in poverty has a very strong negative relantionship, meaning that most people who smokes it is not in poverty.
